Screen Printing

Overview:

Screen printing is a versatile and popular printing method used for a variety of apparel items, including shirts, shorts, and hoodies. It involves transferring ink onto the fabric through a fine mesh screen with a design stencil.

Pros:

  • Vibrant and Durable Prints
  • Works well for large quantities

Cons:

  • Requires separate screens for each color
  • Not ideal for intricate designs

Heat Press: Pros

Heat Press - In-Depth

How it Works:


Heat press is a versatile method ideal for personalization and small runs. Here's how it works:
Design Printing: Your design is printed onto special heat transfer paper using a high-quality printer. Positioning: Place the printed design on the gear where you want the customization. Heat Application: Use a heat press machine to apply high heat and pressure, transferring the design from the paper onto the gear. Cooling: Allow the gear to cool, and the design becomes permanent.

Embroidery: Pros

Screen Printing - In-Depth

How it Works:


Embroidery adds a classy, textured look to sports gear. The process involves these steps:
Digitization: Your design is converted into a digital embroidery file that dictates the stitching pattern. Hoop and Stitch: The gear is hooped, and the embroidery machine follows the digital pattern, stitching the design onto the fabric. Trimming: After embroidery is complete, excess threads are trimmed, and any final touches are made.
